TEAM

OVERVIEW

We are as eclectic as you can get.

Distributed between Havana, London, New York & Sao Paulo, each member speaks fluent Spanish & English.

Our skill sets include: web development, graphic design, videography, photography & content writing.

HELLO. BUENOS DIAS.

HENRIK

Co-Founder / Marketing Director

After a short visit in 2009, he returned 5 years later for a 9-month Spanish course at the University of Havana & never left.

Henrik seeks to share his passion for Cuba with young millennials around the world through direct outreach both on the ground in Havana & through popular social media channels.

KRISTEN

Co-Founder / Director of Editorials & Partnership

She shares her experiences of this wonderful country through her insights on Cuban culture & Havana tips.

Kristen’s Cubsesión began in June 2015. A native New Yorker, the energy & hustle of the capital reminded her of home.

JAMES

Co-Founder / Creative Director

James has lived in Havana for over one year, no small feat for a young Brit.

Intimately familiar with the island, Cuban language, culture and art, James brings his skills in photography, videography and graphic design to bring the already colourful Havana street scenes to life.

DAVID

Production Assistant

David is your music & arts scene go-to guy.

He has a great eye for new events, styles & sounds, capturing all these experiences with his camera, GoPro and iPhone.

GABO

Content Development Manager

A Cuban-born graphic designer with over 10 years of experience, he has worked for a number of local & foreign clients, including the UN, through his company Maharetto Creativos.

Gabriel has recently opened a brick-and-mortar design studio in Central Havana, where he has increased his capacity for onsite printing, photo & video editing using Adobe suites.

HENRY

Product Manager

A British entrepreneur with experience in South America & across Europe, he specialises as a UX/UI Desginer, as well as managing web & app development.

Lost & confused in Havana in 2009 - and with a burning hatred for traditional tourism - he wants foreign visitors to discover real Cuba (and real Cubans).
